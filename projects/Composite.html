Composite Control Arm for Vehicles

Download Project Report (PDF)

Project Overview

This project explores the design, analysis, and manufacturing feasibility of a composite control arm for vehicle suspension systems, replacing traditional steel or aluminum arms with a lighter, rust-resistant, and high-performance alternative. The composite design leverages epoxy resin and glass fiber to achieve significant weight reduction, enhanced durability, and improved mechanical properties.

Objectives

  • Design a composite control arm with optimal strength, stiffness, and weight.
  • Analyze mechanical behavior under real-world loading (turning, acceleration, bending).
  • Evaluate manufacturing processes for efficiency and cost-effectiveness.
  • Compare performance vs. conventional steel/aluminum arms.

Engineering Approach

  • Load Analysis: Studied control arm under turning, acceleration, and bending using F = ma and structural formulas.
  • Material Selection: Chose S-2 Glass Fiber (density 2.46g/cc) & Epoxy Resin for high strength and durability.
  • Lamination Sequence: Tested [03/904]S stack (plies at 0°/90°) for isotropic strength and easy manufacturability.
  • Calculations: Performed stress, strain, and bending calculations for different loading scenarios; compared elongation and shear.
  • Production Method: Selected overbraiding for prototyping (targeting 65% fiber volume fraction for best mechanical results).

Key Results

  • Lighter control arm with high stiffness and rust resistance.
  • Stress under turn: -0.44 MPa (inward centripetal force)
  • Stress under acceleration: -0.59 MPa (forward force)
  • Bending moment: 1291 Nm; significant improvement over metal design.
  • Best performance with [03/904]S stacking at 65% fiber volume fraction.

Conclusion

The composite control arm achieves comparable or better performance than traditional arms, offering superior weight savings, manufacturability, and resistance to corrosion. Future work may involve full-scale testing, lifecycle analysis, and potential application to broader vehicle components.
